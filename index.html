Standard Curriculum

Kinder (3-6) Years

focus on foundational skills like language development (listening, speaking, reading, writing), early math concepts (counting, shapes, patterns), social-emotional learning (sharing, cooperation, self-regulation), and gross/fine motor skills (running, jumping, drawing, building).

Elementary School

focuses on expanding core subjects like language arts (reading, writing, grammar), mathematics (arithmetic, algebra, geometry), science (life, physical, earth), social studies (history, geography, civics), and incorporates art, music, and physical education.

Middle (10-16) Years

focuses on deepening core subjects with increased rigor, including algebra, geometry, biology, chemistry, world history, and literature. It also emphasizes social-emotional development, critical thinking, and preparing students for high school.
